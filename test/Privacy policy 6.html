The New York Times Privacy Policy explains how the company collects, uses, and shares your information when you use their services. These services include their website (NYTimes.com), newspaper, newsletters, and other applications.

Personal Information: This includes details like your email address, demographic information (such as age and ZIP code), and payment details when you subscribe, make purchases, or register for an account. If you link your social media accounts (like Facebook or Google) to your NYTimes account, they’ll also receive some of that information. Non-Personal Information: This is gathered through technology, like cookies and tracking tools, and includes data like your IP address, browsing history, and device details.

The New York Times uses your information to provide and improve their services, customize your experience, and show you relevant advertising. They also use the data for statistical analysis, to understand their audience better, and to inform advertisers about user demographics.

Within The New York Times: Your email won't be sold or rented out without your permission. However, they might share your information with other companies they partner with or acquire in the future. Third Parties: They may share non-personal information (like aggregated data) with advertisers or third-party service providers who help them with billing, email distribution, or ad management. If you participate in events or contests, they might share your info with event sponsors. Legal Compliance: They might disclose your information if required by law or to protect their rights.

The New York Times complies with email regulations and gives you the option to unsubscribe from newsletters and marketing emails. You can also opt out of direct mail or phone promotions.

They take reasonable steps to protect your information, but no method of online storage is 100% secure.

The New York Times generally doesn’t collect information from children under 13, except in certain areas like their Learning Network blog, where kids can post comments under specific guidelines.

If you live in California, you can opt out of sharing your information with third parties for marketing purposes.

The privacy policy may change from time to time. Significant changes will be posted on their website, and registered users will be notified via email.
